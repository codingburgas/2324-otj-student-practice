			Fight Club is a cult classic and satirical film.
			It was released in 1999. Directed by David Fincher.
			Based on the novel by Chuck Palahniuk. The story follows an unnamed narrator, played by Edward Norton.
			He becomes disillusioned with his mundane life. He forms an underground fighting club with Tyler Durden, played by Brad Pitt.
			The movie explores themes of masculinity and consumerism. It has a twist ending that challenges the viewer's perception of reality.
			Fight Club has gained a devoted following over the years. It is praised for its stylish direction and thought-provoking commentary.
